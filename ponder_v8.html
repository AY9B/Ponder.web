Mental Health Resources

Educational information only The information below is drawn from peer-reviewed research and authoritative health organisations. It is intended for educational purposes and does not replace professional medical advice. If you're struggling, please speak to a qualified mental health professional.

🧠 Anxiety Disorders

Types: Generalised Anxiety Disorder (GAD), Social Anxiety, Panic Disorder

Anxiety disorders involve persistent, excessive worry that interferes with daily activities. Symptoms include restlessness, rapid heartbeat, difficulty concentrating, and sleep disturbances.

Evidence-Based Advice: Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) shows large effect sizes for GAD and moderate effects for social anxiety and panic disorder across a meta-analysis of 41 placebo-controlled trials (Carpenter et al., 2018). For social anxiety specifically, CBT produced a large effect on social anxiety symptoms (g = 0.74) compared with controls (Šago et al., 2022). Slow paced breathing and progressive muscle relaxation are commonly used adjuncts; a structured course with a clinician is the most reliably effective approach.
Sources & further reading
  • Carpenter et al. (2018), Depression and Anxiety — PubMed
  • van Dis et al. (2020), JAMA Psychiatry — long-term outcomes
  • NIMH: Anxiety Disorders
  • Anxiety and Depression Association of America

💙 Depression & Low Mood

Depression is characterised by persistent sadness, loss of interest, changes in appetite or sleep, fatigue, and feelings of worthlessness.

Evidence-Based Advice: A network meta-analysis of 218 randomised trials found that walking, jogging, yoga, and strength training meaningfully reduce depressive symptoms (Noetel et al., 2024, BMJ). An umbrella review reported a moderate overall effect for exercise (SMD = -0.67) with a number-needed-to-treat of about 2.78 (2024, J. Affective Disorders). Behavioural activation — scheduling enjoyable or meaningful activities even when unmotivated — has consistent support, and combining therapy with medication generally outperforms either alone for moderate-to-severe depression.
Sources & further reading
  • Noetel et al. (2024), BMJ — network meta-analysis on exercise
  • Kvam et al. (2016), J. Affective Disorders — PubMed
  • APA: Depression
  • WHO: Depression fact sheet

⚡ Chronic Stress & Burnout

Prolonged stress leading to physical and emotional exhaustion, cynicism, and reduced professional efficacy. The WHO classifies burnout as an occupational phenomenon rather than a medical condition.

Evidence-Based Advice: Eight-week Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) programmes produce significant reductions in stress, anxiety and depression in healthy populations (Khoury et al., 2015). A systematic review and meta-analysis on physician burnout found that both individual-focused and structural/organisational interventions reduce burnout, with combined approaches working best (West et al., 2016, Lancet). Adequate sleep (7–9 hours for adults) and clear boundaries between work and rest are foundational; structural changes at work are typically more durable than individual coping strategies alone.
Sources & further reading
  • Khoury et al. (2015), J. Psychosomatic Research — MBSR meta-analysis
  • West et al. (2016), The Lancet — interventions for physician burnout
  • WHO: Burn-out as an occupational phenomenon
  • APA: Stress resources

😴 Sleep Disturbances & Insomnia

Difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or waking too early, leading to daytime fatigue and impaired functioning.

Evidence-Based Advice: Cognitive Behavioural Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I) is the recommended first-line treatment (Perlis et al., 2022). A meta-analysis of 87 RCTs (3,724 patients) found large effects on insomnia severity (g = 0.98) and sleep efficiency (g = 0.71) (van Straten et al., 2018), and a long-term follow-up trial found those gains held at one and ten years (Jernelöv et al., 2022). Practical pillars: a consistent sleep–wake schedule, dimmer light in the hour before bed, a cool bedroom (around 16–19°C / 60–67°F), and getting out of bed if you're awake for more than ~20 minutes.
Sources & further reading
  • van Straten et al. (2018), Sleep Medicine Reviews — CBT-I meta-analysis (PDF)
  • Perlis et al. (2022) — PMC
  • American Academy of Sleep Medicine
  • National Sleep Foundation

🍽️ Eating Disorders

Serious conditions including anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, and binge eating disorder involving complex relationships with food, weight and body image. Eating disorders carry serious medical risks and benefit greatly from early professional support.

Evidence-Based Advice: For adolescents, both Family-Based Treatment (FBT) and Enhanced CBT (CBT-E) lead to similar improvements in eating disorder outcomes (Le Grange et al., 2020). A meta-analysis of 44 effectiveness studies (3,251 patients) reported large effect sizes for eating-disorder psychopathology and weight restoration in anorexia nervosa (2024, Eur. Child Adolesc. Psychiatry). Recovery typically involves a multidisciplinary team — therapist, dietitian, and physician. If you or someone you know may be struggling, reach out to a qualified clinician rather than self-managing.
Sources & further reading
  • Le Grange et al. (2020) — FBT vs CBT-E in adolescents
  • Murphy et al. (2010), Psychiatric Clinics N. America — CBT for eating disorders
  • National Alliance for Eating Disorders (helpline)
  • Academy for Eating Disorders

🚭 Substance Misuse

Harmful or hazardous use of alcohol or drugs that interferes with health, relationships, or daily functioning.

Evidence-Based Advice: Motivational Interviewing has 25+ years of evidence supporting its use across substance use behaviours (Lundahl et al., 2010). For opioid use disorder, medications such as methadone, buprenorphine, and naltrexone substantially reduce illicit use, overdose risk and mortality, and are recognised as standard of care by NIDA. Recovery is generally non-linear — relapse is common and best treated as information for adjusting the plan, not as failure.
Sources & further reading
  • Lundahl et al. (2010), Research on Social Work Practice — Motivational Interviewing meta-analysis
  • NIDA: Medications for opioid use disorder
  • SAMHSA — treatment locator and helpline
  • SMART Recovery — science-based mutual support

🎯 ADHD

Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder involves persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, or impulsivity that interfere with functioning across more than one setting.

Evidence-Based Advice: The landmark NIMH-funded Multimodal Treatment Study (MTA) found that combining medication with behavioural interventions produced the strongest outcomes for children with ADHD (MTA Cooperative Group, 1999). A systematic review and meta-analysis of RCTs found physical exercise improves attention, hyperactivity and related symptoms in children with ADHD (Cerrillo-Urbina et al., 2015). External structure helps — visible timers, checklists, breaking large tasks into small steps — and adequate sleep is consistently linked to better symptom regulation.
Sources & further reading
  • MTA Cooperative Group (1999), Archives of General Psychiatry
  • Cerrillo-Urbina et al. (2015), Child: Care, Health and Development
  • CHADD — Children and Adults with ADHD
  • NIMH: ADHD

🤝 Loneliness & Social Isolation

A perceived lack of meaningful connection with others. Loneliness is a near-universal human experience and not a personal failing.

Evidence-Based Advice: A meta-analysis of more than 3.4 million participants found loneliness associated with a 26% increase in risk of premature death, social isolation 29%, and living alone 32% (Holt-Lunstad et al., 2015). The 2023 US Surgeon General's Advisory frames social connection as a public-health priority, with practical guidance on strengthening it (US Surgeon General, 2023). Quality of relationships matters more than quantity; shared-activity settings (volunteering, classes, hobby groups) tend to build connection more reliably than passive socialising. Note: the popular comparison of loneliness to "smoking 15 cigarettes a day" comes from Holt-Lunstad et al. (2010) but has been debated in subsequent research, so treat as a rough heuristic rather than a precise figure.
Sources & further reading
  • Holt-Lunstad et al. (2015), Perspectives on Psychological Science — PubMed
  • Holt-Lunstad et al. (2010), PLOS Medicine — original mortality meta-analysis
  • US Surgeon General's Advisory on Loneliness (2023)
  • Campaign to End Loneliness (UK)

💔 Self-Harm & Suicidal Ideation

Thoughts of self-harm or suicide can feel overwhelming — please know that support is available, and these feelings, however intense, can change with the right help.

Evidence-Based Advice: Dialectical Behaviour Therapy (DBT) has strong evidence for reducing self-harm and suicidal behaviour, originating with Linehan's foundational two-year RCT showing roughly a 50% reduction in suicidal behaviour (Linehan et al., 2006). A meta-analysis of DBT for adolescents (1,673 participants) found small-to-moderate effects on self-harm and suicidal ideation (Kothgassner et al., 2021). Working with a therapist on a personalised safety plan — recognising warning signs, listing reasons for living, agreed coping strategies, and people to contact — is one of the most evidence-based protective steps. If you're in crisis right now, please contact a helpline below.
Sources & further reading
  • Linehan et al. (2006), Archives of General Psychiatry — DBT RCT
  • Kothgassner et al. (2021), Psychological Medicine — PMC
  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (US)
  • International Association for Suicide Prevention — global directory

🔄 OCD

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder involves intrusive, unwanted thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviours or mental acts (compulsions) intended to reduce distress.

Evidence-Based Advice: Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP) is the first-line psychological treatment for OCD; roughly 50–60% of patients who complete ERP show clinically significant improvement, with gains generally maintained long-term (Law & Boisseau, 2019). A meta-analysis of 21 trials (1,113 patients) found ERP combined with medication outperformed medication alone (2022, Frontiers in Psychiatry). Telehealth ERP also works: an online video-therapy study reported a 43.4% reduction in OCD symptoms with 62.9% classified as full responders (Feusner et al., 2022). Reassurance-seeking tends to reinforce the cycle, so ERP is best done with a clinician trained in the approach.
Sources & further reading
  • Law & Boisseau (2019) — ERP for OCD
  • Feusner et al. (2022), JMIR — telehealth ERP
  • International OCD Foundation
  • ADAA: OCD

🌪️ PTSD

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder can develop after exposure to traumatic events, causing intrusive memories, avoidance, negative shifts in mood, and hyperarousal.

Evidence-Based Advice: Eye Movement Desensitisation and Reprocessing (EMDR) has efficacy established across more than 30 published RCTs and is recommended as a first-line PTSD treatment by international clinical practice guidelines (de Jongh et al., 2024). A meta-analysis of 26 RCTs found significant improvement across PTSD, depression and anxiety symptoms (Chen et al., 2014). A 2025 systematic review found EMDR comparable in effectiveness to trauma-focused CBT and the most cost-effective option (Simpson et al., 2025). Healing isn't linear — most evidence-based courses are time-limited and structured, ideally with a qualified trauma-trained therapist.
Sources & further reading
  • de Jongh et al. (2024), J. Traumatic Stress — state of the science on EMDR
  • Chen et al. (2014), PLOS One — EMDR meta-analysis
  • US Dept. of Veterans Affairs — PTSD resources
  • International Society for Traumatic Stress Studies

🪞 Body Dysmorphia & Low Self-Esteem

Body Dysmorphic Disorder (BDD) involves intense preoccupation with perceived flaws in appearance that others typically can't see or consider minor.

Evidence-Based Advice: A randomised controlled trial of modular CBT specifically designed for BDD showed significant reductions in symptom severity compared with a waitlist control (Wilhelm et al., 2014). Reviews of social media and body image find consistent associations between certain types of social-media use and body dissatisfaction, especially appearance-focused, comparison-heavy use (Fardouly & Vartanian, 2016). Practical levers backed by the literature: reducing mirror-checking, curating feeds away from idealised imagery, and building self-evaluation cues that aren't appearance-based.
Sources & further reading
  • Wilhelm et al. (2014), Behavior Therapy — modular CBT for BDD
  • Fardouly & Vartanian (2016), Current Opinion in Psychology
  • International OCD Foundation — BDD
  • BDD Foundation

🏠 Homesickness

Distress caused by separation from home — common among students, travellers, and people who've recently relocated.

Evidence-Based Advice: Most homesickness improves substantially within the first 6–8 weeks of adjustment in university students (Thurber & Walton, 2012). A systematic review of the homesickness literature highlights protective factors: prior preparation, established routines, social-network building in the new environment, and maintaining manageable contact with home (Stroebe et al., 2015). Feeling homesick is a normal adjustment response; the goal is usually not to suppress it but to add new sources of belonging alongside the old ones.
Sources & further reading
  • Thurber & Walton (2012), J. American College Health
  • Stroebe et al. (2015), Review of General Psychology
  • American College Health Association
  • The Jed Foundation

💰 Financial Stress

Worry about money, debt, and financial security has well-documented effects on both mental and physical health.

Evidence-Based Advice: A study of US adults found a clear, dose-response association between financial worries and psychological distress (Ryu & Fan, 2023). The emerging field of financial therapy combines practical money-management with the emotional and behavioural side of money decisions (Klontz et al., 2008). Concrete steps with the strongest support: visibility (a simple budget you actually look at), automation of bills and savings, and free, accredited counselling for debt — all of which reduce the cognitive load that drives financial anxiety.
Sources & further reading
  • Ryu & Fan (2023), J. Family and Economic Issues
  • Klontz et al. (2008), J. Financial Therapy
  • National Foundation for Credit Counseling
  • Consumer Financial Protection Bureau
